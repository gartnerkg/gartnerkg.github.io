It was a phishing test..

Dear colleague, You are receiving this mail from me because you unfortunately clicked on the LINK in a Gartner internal phishing mail and due to that you failed the test. The link you clicked was sent by a Gartner test system - you should have recognized this as phishing or fake mail.

Your click could have brought our company to a cyber incident or service-outage in the event of a real attack. As you can see, it can happen too fast and the attacker already has an access to the data or to our system. Since it is obviously not yet clear what kind of threat such cyber-attacks can pose, we ask you again to complete this course in e-learning. IT Security & Privacy Course.

If the course is not completed in the next 7 days, we will restrict your internet access to a minimum and inform your supervisor. We will continue to send such internal test mails - if you are not sure about a mail - better ask more questions or send the mail to helpdesk@gartnerkg.com. IT security concerns us all - it is better to ask too many questions than to open or click on the wrong thing!

In case of another "wrong click", IT will arrange a direct conversation with you and your supervisor and temporarily isolate the account completely for security reasons. (only internal mails, no internet, teams etc.) Please do not hesitate to contact us if you have any questions.

Please use this information so that you do not fall for such attacks in the future, whether in the company or in private life. Thank you for your support!

FAQ

You are receiving this email because,
you clicked on a link in a Gartner internal phishing test email,
which resulted in a failed test

  • What was the link I clicked on?
    The link you clicked was part of a phishing test sent by a Gartner test system, designed to help employees recognize phishing attempts.
  • What are the potential consequences of clicking on a phishing link?
    Clicking on a phishing link could lead to a cyber incident or service outage, as attackers may gain access to sensitive data or systems.
  • What should I do now?
    We encourage you to complete the IT Security & Privacy Course within the next 7 days to enhance your understanding of cybersecurity.
  • What happens if I don’t complete the course in time?
    If the course is not completed within 7 days, your internet access will be restricted, and your supervisor will be informed.
  • Will there be more phishing tests in the future?
    Yes, we will continue to send internal test emails. If you are unsure about an email, please ask questions or forward it to helpdesk@gartnerkg.com.
  • What should I do if I suspect a phishing attempt?
    If you are uncertain about an email, it is better to ask questions than to click on any links. Always prioritize caution.
  • What happens if I click on a phishing link again?
    In the event of another "wrong click," IT will arrange a direct conversation with you and your supervisor, and your account may be temporarily isolated for security reasons.
  • Who can I contact if I have questions?
    Please do not hesitate to reach out if you have any questions or need further clarification. Your understanding of these issues is important for everyone's security.
  • How can I protect myself from phishing attacks in the future?
    Use the information provided to help you recognize and avoid phishing attempts, both in the workplace and in your personal life. Thank you for your support!
